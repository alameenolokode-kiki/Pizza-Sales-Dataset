# Pizza-Sales-Dataset
The dataset used in this project contains records of pizza sales made by a restaurant over a period of time. Each record represents a single pizza sale and includes information such as the pizza name, pizza category, pizza size, date of sale, and the amount of revenue generated.

  ## Data Cleaning and Processing
Before analysis, the data was cleaned and processed to ensure accuracy and consistency. Duplicate records were removed, missing values were checked, and pizza names, categories, and sizes were standardized. Date fields were converted into a monthly format for trend analysis, and revenue fields were verified as numeric values. These steps were necessary to eliminate errors and ensure reliable results throughout the analysis and visualization process.

- This section describes how raw data was prepared for analysis.

  ### Explanation
The following steps were performed:

-	Removed duplicate entries 

-	Standardized pizza names and categories 

-	Corrected inconsistent size labels 

=	Converted dates into monthly format 

-	Verified revenue values were numeric 

-	Checked for missing or invalid data

- Clean data ensures accurate results and reliable insights.

 ![](https://github.com/alameenolokode-kiki/Pizza-Sales-Dataset/blob/main/Monthy%20sales%20Trend.PNG)

#### What data this chart uses

-	Order Date → grouped by month (January to December) 
-	Total Sales Revenue → summed for each month 
  
#### What this chart really means 

This chart shows how the business performs over time.

- When the line goes up, it means sales are increasing. 

- When the line goes down, it means fewer pizzas were sold or customers spent less. 

-	Peaks show high-demand months. 

-	Dips show slow periods. 

This helps identify seasonal patterns, such as:
-	Busy months (holidays, festivals, promotions) 

-	Slow months (post-holiday or off-season periods) 

#### What the business should work on 
-	Study high-sales months

- What, promotions, pricing, or events caused the increase? 

-	Plan marketing for low-sales months

-	 Discounts, bundles, or special offers 

-	Staff planning

- More staff in peak months, optimized staffing in slow months 

-	Inventory control
  
- Order more ingredients before high-demand periods
